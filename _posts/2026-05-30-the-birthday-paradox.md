How many people do you need in a room for it to be more likely than not
that two of them share a birthday? The answer is surprising: **just 23**.

With 23 people the probability is already above 50%, and with 70 it is
around 99.9%. The key is that we are not comparing each person against a
fixed date, but **every possible pair among them**, and those grow very
quickly.

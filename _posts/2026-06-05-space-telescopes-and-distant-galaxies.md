Modern space telescopes let us observe galaxies whose light has travelled
for billions of years. Each image is, in fact, a window into the past of
the universe.

The fascinating part is that by looking so far away we are looking *back*
in time: we see those galaxies as they were, not as they are now. A good
excuse to remember how big — and how old — all of this is.
